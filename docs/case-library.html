How to read this page

What this page is for

It gives homeowners a clearer sense of what different property situations look like before they spend money on drawings, contractor quoting, or legal assumptions.

What this page is not

It is not a guarantee, not a permit opinion, and not proof that a different property will follow the same outcome. The details still depend on address, jurisdiction, records, and site conditions.

Representative project patterns

Pattern 1: LA City garage conversion flagged during a pending sale

  • Typical situation: A homeowner or agent discovers that a garage was converted years ago and the sale process surfaces a permit or notice concern.
  • First move: Collect the notice, address, deadline, and permit history before anyone says it can “definitely be legalized.”
  • Why screening mattered: In LA City, not every unpermitted unit belongs in the same path, and UDU is not a universal answer.

Pattern 2: Arcadia homeowner exploring a cleaner detached ADU path

  • Typical situation: A homeowner wants to add a backyard unit and has heard Arcadia has clearer ADU entry points or pre-approved pathways.
  • First move: Confirm the address, lot conditions, and project type before assuming a standard or fast path applies.
  • Why screening mattered: Arcadia can be attractive for standard ADU paths, but the project still depends on the site and exact category.

Pattern 3: Monterey Park or San Gabriel property with older work and unclear records

  • Typical situation: An older home has garage-related work, additions, or mismatched records and the owner is not sure whether the issue is renovation, permit cleanup, or legalization.
  • First move: Separate the property into jurisdiction, existing conditions, and permit history instead of treating it like a normal remodel inquiry.
  • Why screening mattered: Older SGV properties often mix ADU questions with legacy issues, so a clean route depends on what the records actually show.

Pattern 4: Pasadena property where city-specific review matters more than assumptions

  • Typical situation: A homeowner wants an ADU or conversion path and assumes Pasadena works like LA City because the mailing address feels similar.
  • First move: Confirm Pasadena jurisdiction and whether additional city-specific or historic constraints may affect the route.
  • Why screening mattered: Pasadena has its own standards and cannot be explained as if it were LA City.

What these patterns all have in common

  • The right first step is usually not “quote the construction.”
  • The right first step is usually not “assume the city will approve it.”
  • The right first step is confirming jurisdiction, project type, record history, and blocker signals.

This is why the front-door offer is a property screen first. Different situations can look similar on the surface and still require different next steps after review.

When to start with the screen

You have a converted garage or old room issue

Especially if a notice, sale, refinance, or old record question has already surfaced.

You want an ADU but do not know the real city path yet

Especially in SGV cities where the public entry is clearer than the actual project fit.

You want to avoid wasting money on the wrong next step

Screening helps sort cleaner-looking projects from cases that need deeper review first.
